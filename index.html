02 / Experience

Two years, three roles, one fraud program

Promoted through all three roles below within two years at Wipro, on a Google-managed trust & safety program. Recognized with a Best Reviewer Award.

  1. Dec 2021 – Dec 2022 Wipro (Client: Google) · India

    Business Intelligence & Payments Risk Analyst

    • Analyzed 100+ transactions per case across an average of 60 flagged Google Wallet accounts daily (300/week, 15,000+ over 12 months), using transaction-monitoring and behavioral-anomaly signals to detect account takeover, refund/policy abuse, and payment-method fraud — including chargeback and dispute review when compromised accounts led to unauthorized charges.
    • Reviewed customer-submitted government-issued ID and payment-card documentation for post-closure account appeals, distinguishing genuine from fake or forged submissions, then applied the appropriate case action and communicated decisions in formal written guidance to support agents.
    • Extended fraud-review protocols to Google Store hardware launches (Pixel 7, Pixel Buds, Nest) and YouTube Premium subscription transactions, applying billing/shipping-address verification and payment-fraud detection logic.
    • Identified and escalated data-quality issues in case-management workflows, improving fraud-analytics reliability.
  2. Dec 2022 – Jun 2023 Wipro (Client: Google)

    Subject Matter Expert

    • Promoted through competitive internal selection to audit escalated cases across a 20-person team, reviewing account history and prior analyst reasoning to correct misclassifications.
    • Conducted 1:1 quality-check reviews of associate case decisions, and led onboarding and shadowing for newly onboarded analysts.
    • Standardized review logic and detection-policy communication across the team. Shortlisted for Team Lead (B2) based on performance.
  3. Jun 2023 – Dec 2023 Wipro (Client: Google) · GTFF

    Fraud Pattern Analyst, GTFF

    • Analyzed raw account-level data across Google Wallet, Google Store, and YouTube transactions within GTFF — a centralized trust & fraud analytics function — to identify deeper fraud patterns beyond individual case review.
    • Examined payment methods, devices, and banking sources to surface emerging fraud typologies, and worked directly with Google engineers to feed those findings into automated detection models — improving detection speed for newly identified fraud patterns.

NYC Air Quality Analytics & Forecasting

15 years of NYC Department of Health air-quality data (~18,800 records, 59 community districts), focused on NO₂ and PM2.5. Cleaned in Excel and Power Query, analyzed with statistical hypothesis testing in Python, modeled with Decision Tree, Random Forest, and Linear Regression in RapidMiner (R² up to 0.78). Delivered as a multi-page Power BI dashboard and infographic poster, presented live to 90+ people.

Switch the season on the chart — it redraws with the real annual, winter, and summer figures, and the site's accent shifts with it.
